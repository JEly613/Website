## Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's three laws form the foundation of classical mechanics. They describe the relationship between forces acting on a body and its motion.

### First Law: Inertia

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

### Second Law: Force and Acceleration

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. This is expressed as:

$$F = ma$$

where $F$ is the net force, $m$ is the mass, and $a$ is the acceleration.

### Third Law: Action and Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. If object A exerts a force on object B, then object B exerts an equal and opposite force on object A.

## Applications

### Free Fall

When an object falls freely under gravity near Earth's surface, it experiences a constant acceleration $g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$. The position as a function of time is:

$$y(t) = y_0 + v_0 t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

where $y_0$ is the initial position and $v_0$ is the initial velocity.

### Projectile Motion

For a projectile launched at angle $\theta$ with initial speed $v_0$, the horizontal and vertical components of motion are:

$$x(t) = v_0 \cos\theta \cdot t$$

$$y(t) = v_0 \sin\theta \cdot t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

The maximum height reached is $h_{max} = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g}$ and the range is $R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$.
